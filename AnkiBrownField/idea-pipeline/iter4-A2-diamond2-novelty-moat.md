## The claim under audit

Co-present TWO near-identical ethics vignettes differing by ONE decisive fact that flips
conform↔violate; force the learner to NAME the decisive fact; reveal the CFA Standard AFTER the
attempt. Grounded in contrastive-case / analogical-comparison learning (Alfieri 2013 meta d=0.50;
Gentner 2003 adult-professional 48% vs 19% transfer).

Decompose it into 4 separable components — each must be checked for prior art separately:
1. **Contrastive/minimal-pair mechanism** (compare two near-identical cases).
2. **"One decisive fact flips the answer"** framing for exam prep.
3. **Applied to CFA ethics Standard-discrimination** specifically.
4. **Packaged as an SRS drill** (co-present pair → force naming the fact → delayed Standard reveal).

## Q1 — IS IT ACTUALLY NEW?

### 1A. The mechanism is heavily PRODUCTIZED — in adjacent domains

- **Language minimal pairs = a mature product category.** Multiple shipping apps train perceptual
  discrimination via ABX minimal-pair drills with adaptive "focus on your weakest contrast"
  scheduling and instant feedback: [minimalpairs.co](https://minimalpairs.co/),
  [Soundwise](https://getsoundwise.co/) (40+ contrasts, adaptive, one-time purchase),
  [Vocaber "Minimal Pairs" on Google Play](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?hl=en_US&id=com.vocaber.minimal_pairs).
  → The **exact "minimal pair" framing, the adaptive weakest-contrast loop, and the
  hear-both-side-by-side-then-choose interaction are already commercial** — just phonetic, not
  conceptual. **Grade of prior-art match: A (productized), different domain.**
- **"Contrasting cases" is a named, taught study technique** in consumer learning content, citing
  the same Gentner/Schwartz literature this diamond rests on:
  [Highbrow lesson "Contrasting Cases"](https://gohighbrow.com/contrasting-cases/),
  [Cult of Pedagogy "Contrasting Cases"](https://www.cultofpedagogy.com/contrasting-cases/)
  ("create examples that differ in one important way"). → The idea is public, popularized, and
  explicitly tied to the founding research. **Not a secret.**

### 1B. The MOST damaging hit — "one differing fact flips the answer" is ALREADY a shipping ethics-exam-prep tool

- **[mdsteps.com — USMLE Ethics Decision Tree](https://mdsteps.com/ethics)** (Step 2 CK / Step 3):
  interactive tool whose stated use is: *"Use the Back button to explore alternate paths and see how
  a small change in the vignette (e.g., **capacity vs no capacity, minor vs adult**) **flips the best
  answer**."* It is explicitly "designed to mimic how exam writers think" for medical ethics.
  → This is Diamond #2's core insight — **one decisive fact flips conform↔violate, productized for a
  professional-ethics licensing exam** — already live. It is a branching decision tree with a
  Back button, NOT co-presented paired vignettes with forced fact-naming + SRS, so it is not an
  exact clone, but it defeats any claim that "one-fact-flips-the-answer ethics drilling" is novel.
  **Grade: strong prior art (productized, professional-ethics exam, near-identical insight).**
- Medical-ethics pedagogy also formally uses paired contrasting cases and "boundary cases" to force
  cognitive conflict: [Ethics Workup, Baylor PA program](https://doi.org/10.1097/01367895-200718010-00006)
  ("Two case vignettes … progressing in complexity"); [Bursten & Finkelstein, "Promoting Cognitive
  Conflict in Health Care Ethics"](https://uknowledge.uky.edu/philosophy_facpub/8) (boundary cases →
  productive cognitive conflict). Pedagogy, not product, but confirms the pattern is standard in the
  ethics-teaching world.

### 1C. In CFA ethics specifically — the "decisive fact" + "confusable Standard pairs" is UNIVERSAL, EXPLICIT pedagogy

Every major CFA prep source already teaches the exact cognitive move this diamond automates, applied
to single vignettes, and several **publish the confusable-Standard pairs as tables**:

- **[300hours CFA Ethics Cheat Sheet](https://300hours.com/cfa-level-1-ethics-cheat-sheet/)** —
  *"Trap 3: Confusing similar Standards. Watch for scenarios that test your ability to distinguish
  between:"* then lists explicit pairs: **I(A) Knowledge of Law vs I(D) Misconduct · III(A) Loyalty
  to Clients vs IV(A) Loyalty to Employer · VI(A) Disclosure vs VI(C) Referral Fees · II(A) MNPI vs
  III(E) Confidentiality.** → The **exact confusable clusters this deck would author are already
  enumerated publicly.** This is the content moat, pre-broken by a free cheat sheet.
- **[Analyst Mastery L1](https://analystmastery.com/cfa-level-1/ethical-and-professional-standards/guidance-for-standards-i-vii-in-scenarios/)
  and [L2](https://analystmastery.com/cfa-level-2/ethical-and-professional-standards/code-standards-and-the-level-ii-ethics-frame/)** —
  a literal decision flow: *"What fact actually changes the ethics answer? → Which standard is
  primary? → Did the conduct conform or violate?"* and *"by embedding one decisive fact inside a long
  realistic story."* This is Diamond #2's pedagogy, verbatim, minus the paired presentation.
- **[testprepinsight 2026 CFA L1 Ethics guide](https://testprepinsight.com/resources/cfa-level-1-ethics/)** —
  teaches the Mosaic Theory near-miss as a contrast: *what IS allowed (combine non-material nonpublic
  + material public) vs what is NOT (trade on a single material nonpublic fact)* … *"Learn to
  recognize the difference between these two scenarios, because you'll likely be tested on it."*
  → A prose minimal pair. The only thing missing is co-presentation + forced naming + SRS.
- **[AcadiFi](https://acadifi.com/knowledge/cfa-ethics-standards-duty-decision-map)** and Kaplan
  Schweser both push "identify the decisive fact before the answer choices"; Schweser's own QBank is
  organized by LOS for targeted single-item repetition.
- **[CFA Society LA L2 Ethics deck (K. Yee)](https://www.cfala.org/files/KYee_L2_Ethics_24.pdf)** —
  *"a possible Standards violation may be spotted by a single word within the hypothetical (e.g.
  'none', 'quickly')."* The "one decisive word/fact" idea is taught down to the token.

**Does any CFA provider ship co-presented near-miss PAIRS?** No product found. **UWorld's**
signature differentiator is *"why every wrong answer is wrong"* — within-question distractor
contrast, not two near-identical vignettes ([UWorld vs Meldrum](https://finance.uworld.com/cfa/uworld-cfa-vs-mark-meldrum-cfa/),
[300hours CFA apps](https://300hours.com/top-cfa-apps/)). That is a *cousin* (contrast the options),
not the mechanism (contrast the scenarios). No CFA QBank found that presents Variant-A vs Variant-B
of the same stem and asks the learner to name the flipping fact.

### 1D. Anki community — no pre-made contrastive-pair ethics deck exists

Search confirmed there is **no pre-made Anki deck dedicated to the confusable CFA ethics pairs**;
existing decks (e.g. [Top Student CFA L1 deck on TpT](https://www.teacherspayteachers.com/Product/CFA-Level-1-Anki-Flashcards-FULL-12822001))
are conventional definition/Q-A cards. The Anki forum's standard advice for confusable items is to
make a single card *"focussing on the differences"* between the pair
([AnkiWeb forums](https://forums.ankiweb.net/t/how-do-i-deal-with-pairs-of-cards-that-i-confuse-for-each-other/55515)).
So the DIY pattern is folk knowledge; the packaged drill is not shipped.

### 1E. Generic ed-tech "contrasting cases / compare-contrast" — described, not shipped for ethics

- [eduKate "Learning by Contrasts Using AI"](https://edukatesingapore.com/2025/07/02/learning-by-contrasts-using-ai/)
  name-drops Smart Sparrow / DreamBox generating "contrasting scenarios" — vague marketing, no
  ethics product. **UNVERIFIED** as a real shipping feature.
- [GamED-AI (GitHub research)](https://github.com/ShivenA99/GamED-AI) lists **"compare-contrast"**
  and **"branching-scenario"** as auto-generated game mechanics — research pipeline, not a product,
  but shows the mechanic is a known, automatable primitive.
- [Studeia adaptive-learning docs](https://docs.studeia.com/features/ai-tutor/adaptive-learning)
  ship a misconception lifecycle + inline quizzes, but not paired contrastive vignettes.

### NOVELTY VERDICT: **INCREMENTAL** (leaning ALREADY-EXISTS-in-parts)

- The **mechanism** is productized (language minimal pairs = A-grade prior art; medical-ethics
  "one-fact-flips-the-answer" tool = strong prior art via mdsteps.com).
- The **"decisive fact" + confusable-Standard-pair content** is universal, explicit, publicly
  tabulated CFA-prep pedagogy (300hours, Analyst Mastery, testprepinsight, CFA Society decks).
- The **exact packaging** — co-present two CFA ethics vignettes + force naming the flipping fact +
  delayed Standard reveal, inside an SRS — is **not found as a shipping product**. That specific
  assembly is genuinely un-shipped.
- Net: this is a **novel ASSEMBLY of entirely non-novel parts.** Not GENUINELY NEW (every component
  is prior art, one of them productized in a sibling exam-prep domain); not ALREADY EXISTS (no exact
  clone). → **INCREMENTAL.**

## Q2 — IF IT'S SO GOOD, WHY HASN'T A COMPETITOR DONE IT?

Classify against the offered options: (a) authoring near-miss PAIRS is expensive/hard, (b) they
don't think in learning-science terms, (c) business-model, (d) legal/content, (e) NO GOOD REASON.

- **NOT (d) legal/content.** The Code & Standards is CFA-Institute IP but the confusable clusters and
  the "decisive fact" method are freely published (see 300hours). No legal barrier beyond writing
  original vignettes — which every provider already does.
- **Partly (b) they don't think in learning-science terms — but weakly.** They clearly DO think in
  discrimination terms (they publish the confusable-pair tables and the "what fact changes the
  answer" flowcharts). What they haven't done is take the last step from *teaching the heuristic in
  prose* to *building it as a forced-comparison drill*. That's a product-design gap, not an
  ignorance gap. So (b) is real but shallow.
- **Partly (c) business-model.** Incumbents monetize **QBank volume + video hours** (UWorld =
  question-first bank + explanations; Meldrum = lectures; Schweser = LOS-tagged QBank). A
  paired-vignette drill produces *fewer, more expensive-to-author* items and doesn't inflate a
  "10,000 questions" marketing number. Mild disincentive, not a wall.
- **Mostly (a) authoring the PAIRS is the real cost — i.e., a CONTENT MOAT.** A good minimal pair
  requires two vignettes identical except for one fact that cleanly flips the verdict, with the
  distractor Standards controlled. That is genuine SME work and is the only thing standing between a
  competitor and this feature.
- **And a strong dose of (e) NO GOOD REASON.** The confusable clusters are already public. Every
  incumbent already owns a large ethics QBank and (per their own marketing) an LLM-assisted authoring
  pipeline. Converting existing single vignettes into pairs is a *format transform*, not new
  research. mdsteps already shipped the harder conceptual version for medical ethics. **There is no
  technical, data, or distribution barrier — only the willingness to author pairs.**

### Is the only moat "someone has to write the pairs"? → YES. And it's a WEAK content moat.

- **Pure content moat.** By design, Diamond #2 is deterministic, zero new Rust, reuses Diamond #1's
  note-type/authoring toolchain. There is **no technical moat, no data moat, no network effect, no
  switching cost.** The entire defensibility is "we authored good pairs."
- **The content moat is easily out-authored.** The confusable-Standard clusters to author against
  are *published for free* (300hours cheat sheet literally lists them). Any incumbent with an
  existing ethics QBank + LLM drafting can convert to pairs in weeks — faster than a new entrant,
  because they already have the vignettes and the SME reviewers.
- **First-mover advantage is thin.** Nothing stops UWorld/Schweser/AnalystPrep from adding a
  "compare these two scenarios" mode; UWorld is already 80% there conceptually with its
  "why-each-option-is-wrong" engine.

### MOAT VERDICT: **WEAK.** Reason class: **(a) content-authoring cost + (e) no structural reason.**
The only moat is "someone has to write the pairs" — a content moat over publicly-enumerated
clusters, out-authorable by any incumbent QBank vendor. No tech/data/network defensibility exists
(by design).

## Q3 — SAME METRICS RE-CHECK

### Evidence grade (mechanism): unchanged — ≥3 A/B pillars, comfortably
Re-affirmed from `iter3-H1`: **≥5 independent A/B pillars** for the contrastive-comparison mechanism:
Alfieri, Nokes-Malach & Schunn 2013 (meta, d=0.50 [.44,.56], **A−**) · Gentner, Loewenstein &
Thompson 2003 (adult professionals, 48% vs 19%, **A−**) · Corral, Kurtz & Jones 2020 (contrast >
comparison for confusable categories, **B**) · Rittle-Johnson & Star 2007 (RCT, **B/B+**) · Schwartz
et al. 2011 (RCT d=0.31, **B+**). **PASS.** This audit does not weaken the mechanism grade.

### The known asterisk: unchanged, and arguably heavier now
Ethics/legal-rule-application-specific A/B evidence remains **C / UNVERIFIED** — the mechanism is
proven in math, physics, negotiation, and category learning and **transferred to CFA ethics by
analogy.** No study A/B-tests contrastive minimal pairs on CFA-Standard discrimination. The novelty
audit adds a second asterisk: **no efficacy data that the productized paired form beats a standard
LOS-tagged QBank** (the incumbents' single-item method), so the *product* advantage over the status
quo is itself unproven. The transfer profile is also modest (Schwartz d≈0.31 on transfer; ~0 on
rote) — a real but not spectacular expected gain.

### Buildability in 4 days: unchanged — YES (and this cuts against the moat)
Deterministic scoring (two conform/violate calls + which-Standard MCQ), reuses Diamond #1's curated
bank + note-type + offline-draft/human-verify pipeline, **zero new Rust.** Only cost = authoring the
pairs. Buildability is a GO — but the same cheapness that makes it buildable in 4 days makes it
cheap for a competitor to copy. **Buildability and defensibility are inversely related here.**

## OUTPUT — VERDICTS

**NOVELTY VERDICT: INCREMENTAL.**
A novel *assembly* of non-novel parts. The contrastive/minimal-pair mechanism is productized in
language learning (minimalpairs.co, Soundwise, Vocaber) and — decisively — the "one decisive fact
flips the ethics answer" insight is already a shipping professional-ethics exam-prep tool
([mdsteps.com USMLE Ethics Decision Tree](https://mdsteps.com/ethics)). The "decisive fact" +
confusable-Standard-pair content is universal, explicit, publicly-tabulated CFA-prep pedagogy
([300hours cheat sheet](https://300hours.com/cfa-level-1-ethics-cheat-sheet/),
[Analyst Mastery](https://analystmastery.com/cfa-level-1/ethical-and-professional-standards/guidance-for-standards-i-vii-in-scenarios/),
[testprepinsight](https://testprepinsight.com/resources/cfa-level-1-ethics/)). What no one ships is
the specific SRS packaging (co-present pair → force naming the flipping fact → delayed Standard
reveal). That packaging is un-shipped; the ingredients are not.

**MOAT VERDICT: WEAK.** Reason class **(a) content-authoring cost + (e) no structural reason.**
The sole moat is "someone has to author the pairs" — a content moat over clusters that are *already
published for free*, out-authorable by any incumbent that owns an ethics QBank + LLM drafting. By
design there is no technical, data, or network moat. UWorld is conceptually adjacent already.

**BLUNT "STILL A DIAMOND, AND BEATABLE?" CALL:**
Still a legitimate FEATURE, but it is the **weakest of the three diamonds on defensibility, and it
is BEATABLE.** Its evidence pillar (mechanism) and buildability hold — keep it in the product. But
strip the "genuinely new / defensible wedge" narrative: it is INCREMENTAL and its moat is a thin,
publicly-pre-broken content moat. It survives as a diamond **only as part of the bundle** — its real
defensibility comes from (1) being fused into Diamond #1's honesty engine / memory-vs-performance
spine (which incumbents can't trivially replicate), and (2) authoring quality + speed, not from the
idea being unique. If shipped as a standalone "contrastive ethics app," a funded incumbent
out-authors it in a quarter. **Verdict: keep it, but demote its novelty/moat claim and lean on
integration + execution, not originality.**

### Recommended ledger action
- Keep Diamond #2 in the product (evidence A/B mechanism intact, buildable, composes with #1/#3).
- **Downgrade its NOVELTY/MOAT status: novelty INCREMENTAL, moat WEAK (content-only).** It is not a
  standalone defensible wedge.
- Record the two new prior-art asterisks: (i) mdsteps.com already ships the "one-fact-flips-the-
  answer" ethics-exam tool; (ii) confusable-Standard clusters are publicly enumerated (300hours), so
  the content moat is pre-broken.
- Reframe the moat as **"integration into the honesty/retrieval engine + authoring quality/speed,"**
  not "unique mechanism."
